Mama's Munchies

Handmade snacks and treats — traditional recipes, locally sourced ingredients, delivered with love.

Site Name & Branding

Site name: Mama's Munchies

Why this name? Short, memorable, and communicates handcrafted, home-style snacks and warmth — fits a local business selling homemade treats.

Optional domain (suggestion): mamasmunchies.ng (or mamasmunchies.com)

Site Purpose

The website will serve as a small local storefront and marketing hub: show product catalog with prices, accept customer orders via a contact/order form, collect customer reviews, and showcase product photos in an interactive JavaScript gallery. The site will be responsive and optimized for quick load on mobile devices.

  • Primary goals: increase local visibility, collect orders, and present the product range clearly.
  • Secondary goals: collect testimonials and capture email leads for occasional promotions.

Scenarios (target audience questions)

  1. "Do you deliver within my neighborhood and how do I place an order?" — Answer: Delivery area map, order form with contact and pickup/delivery options.
  2. "Which snacks are nut-free and what are your prices?" — Answer: Product catalog page with filters (allergens) and visible pricing.

Color Scheme

#E97F02
#2B2D42
#F8F9FA

Usage:

  • Primary (accent): #E97F02 — buttons, CTAs, highlights, price tags.
  • Headings & nav: #2B2D42 — primary text color for strong contrast.
  • Background: #F8F9FA — page background, cards remain white for readability.

Typography

Selected Google Fonts (kept minimal for clarity):

  • Montserrat — headings, navigation, CTAs (use 600–800 weight).
  • Lora — body copy and product descriptions for a warm, readable tone.

Usage example: H1/H2 — Montserrat; Body — Lora; Buttons — Montserrat (bold).

Pages & Content Outline

  • Home — hero/gallery, featured products, short about, CTA to order.
  • Products — catalog grid, filters (allergens, price), product details, prices.
  • About Us — story, sourcing, small team / contact info.
  • Order / Contact — simple form (name, phone, address, order details), delivery/pickup options.
  • Reviews — customer testimonials, star ratings.

CSS & Implementation Notes

Keep CSS modular: base.css, layout.css, components.css. Use modern CSS (flexbox + grid) and a small JS for gallery and form validation. Make images optimized (compressed WebP) and ensure fast mobile loads.

Testing & Self-Check

Run the following checks during development:

  • HTML validator (W3C) — ensure valid markup.
  • Accessibility: use axe or Lighthouse to check contrast, roles, form labels, and keyboard navigation.
  • SEO: meta title, description, structured data for products where appropriate.
  • Performance: Lighthouse (keep mobile CLS, TTFB and LCP within good ranges), compress images, enable caching.
  • Cross-browser: quick test on Chrome, Firefox, and mobile Safari.
